# Customer-Churn-Analysis
Customer Churn Analysis using the IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset. Analyzed customer behavior, subscription patterns, engagement metrics, and service usage to identify factors contributing to churn and recommend retention strategies.
# Customer Churn Analysis

## Objective

Analyze customer behavior and subscription data to identify the factors that contribute to customer churn and recommend strategies to improve retention.

## Dataset Overview

* Total Customers: 7,043
* Active Customers: 5,174
* Churned Customers: 1,869
* Churn Rate: 26.5%

## Key Findings

### 1. Contract Type Impact

Customers with monthly contracts showed significantly higher churn rates compared to customers with one-year and two-year contracts.

Reason:

* Monthly subscribers can cancel at any time.
* Long-term contracts increase customer commitment.

### 2. Customer Tenure

Customers with shorter tenure were more likely to churn.

Observation:

* New customers are at higher risk of cancellation.
* Long-term customers demonstrate greater loyalty.

### 3. Monthly Charges

Customers paying higher monthly charges showed a higher tendency to churn.

Reason:

* Customers may perceive the service as expensive.
* Competitors may offer lower-cost alternatives.

### 4. Technical Support and Security Services

Customers without Tech Support and Online Security services had higher churn rates.

Reason:

* Lack of support reduces customer satisfaction.
* Customers may face unresolved service issues.

### 5. Paperless Billing

Customers using paperless billing exhibited slightly higher churn rates.

Possible Explanation:

* Digital-first customers may switch providers more easily.

### 6. Senior Citizens

Senior citizens displayed a slightly higher churn tendency than other customer groups.

Reason:

* Different service expectations and usage patterns.

## Behavioral Trends

Characteristics commonly observed among churned customers:

* Short subscription duration
* Month-to-month contracts
* Higher monthly charges
* Limited use of support services
* Lower customer engagement

Characteristics commonly observed among retained customers:

* Longer tenure
* One-year or two-year contracts
* Use of Tech Support
* Use of Online Security services
* Stable monthly spending

## Retention Recommendations

### Recommendation 1

Offer discounts and incentives to encourage customers to move from monthly contracts to annual plans.

### Recommendation 2

Implement onboarding programs for new customers during the first six months.

### Recommendation 3

Provide bundled services such as Online Security and Tech Support.

### Recommendation 4

Identify customers with high monthly charges and provide personalized offers.

### Recommendation 5

Develop loyalty rewards for long-term customers.

## Conclusion

The analysis indicates that contract type, customer tenure, monthly charges, and support services are the primary drivers of customer churn. Customers on month-to-month contracts with higher charges and limited support engagement are most likely to cancel subscriptions. Implementing targeted retention strategies can significantly reduce churn and improve customer satisfaction.
